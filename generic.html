1. Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. It consists of a numerator (top number) and a denominator (bottom number).

Types of Fractions:

  • Proper Fraction: Numerator is smaller than the denominator (e.g., 3/5).
  • Improper Fraction: Numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., 7/4).
  • Mixed Fraction: A whole number and a fraction combined (e.g., 2 1/3).
Example: If a pizza is cut into 8 slices and you eat 3, the fraction of pizza eaten is 3/8.

2. Word Problems

Word problems help apply math concepts to real-life situations. Read the problem carefully, identify key numbers, and use the correct operation.

Example: Sara has 15 apples and gives 5 to her friend. How many apples does she have left? Answer: 10 apples (15 - 5 = 10).

3. Operations

Mathematical operations help in solving problems. The four basic operations are:

  • Addition (+): Combining two or more numbers (e.g., 12 + 8 = 20).
  • Subtraction (-): Taking one number away from another (e.g., 15 - 6 = 9).
  • Multiplication (×): Repeated addition (e.g., 4 × 3 = 12).
  • Division (÷): Splitting a number into equal parts (e.g., 20 ÷ 4 = 5).
Example: If a box has 6 rows of 4 candies each, how many candies are there in total? Answer: 24 (6 × 4 = 24).

4. Percentage

A percentage is a number expressed as a fraction of 100. It is used to compare values easily.

Formula:

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Example: If a test has 50 questions and you answer 40 correctly, your score is:
(40 ÷ 50) × 100 = 80%.

Conclusion

Understanding these math concepts will help you solve problems in daily life and improve your mathematical skills!
